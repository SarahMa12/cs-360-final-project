Mental Health in Tech

By Sarah Ma

Project Description

This project explores the effect of workplace culture on mental health within the tech industry. Using the OSMI Mental Health in Tech Survey dataset, this interactive dashboard allows users to visualize how factors like company size, accessibility to support systems, and demographics impact employee mental health. This project will have a total of 4 visualizations, including a geographic mapping, parallel coordinates, diverging bar chart, and a sunburst diagram.

Even though tech companies exist all over the country, access to mental health resources is not the same everywhere. Some states and cities have stronger healthcare systems, better workplace policies, and more open conversations around mental health, while others still lack support. This map highlights where employees are more likely to have access to care and where gaps in support still exist across the U.S.

Objective: What is the gap in mental health support between small and large companies?

Different company sizes create different workplace experiences. Large companies may have bigger budgets for benefits and wellness programs, but smaller companies can sometimes provide more flexibility and closer personal connections. This chart explores how support changes as companies grow and whether employee well-being keeps up with the fast pace of the tech industry.

Sunburst Diagram - Demographics and Mental Health Treatment

Objective: How do personal and demographics relate to mental health in the tech industry?

Mental health treatment patterns are not the same across all groups. The sunburst chart shows that individuals with a family history of mental health conditions are generally more likely to seek treatment across genders. In contrast, people without a family history, especially males, appear less likely to pursue support. These patterns suggest that personal background and social attitudes may both influence how people respond to mental health challenges and whether they choose to seek help.

Methodology

Data Sources & Processing

The dataset used for this project is the OSMI Mental Health in Tech Survey. Data cleaning was performed using Python (Pandas) to filter for respondents within the United States, normalize gender categories, and handle missing values in workplace-related columns. The final cleaned dataset contains 737 entries.

Metrics & Features

  • Support Score: A metric (0–6) derived by aggregating six key resource categories: Health Benefits, Care Options, Wellness Programs, Help Resources, Anonymity, and Leave Accessibility.
  • Demographics: Age, Cleaned Gender, and Family History of mental health conditions.
  • Workplace Conditions: Company size, remote work status, industry type (Tech vs. Non-tech), and comfort levels discussing mental health with supervisors and coworkers.
  • Outcome Variable: Treatment-seeking status (Yes/No).

Tools & Libraries

  • D3.js (v7): Used for data binding and rendering all SVG-based visualizations.
  • TopoJSON & D3-Geo: Employed for rendering the U.S. choropleth map.
  • D3-Sankey: Used to visualize the flow between workplace conditions and treatment outcomes.
  • D3-Legend: Implemented for color scale and categorical legends.
  • Python (Pandas/NumPy): Used for initial data exploration and preprocessing.

AI Usage

Gemini was used for brainstorming optimal visualization strategies. For example, it recommended transitioning from a parallel coordinates plot to a Sankey diagram to better represent categorical (Yes/No) data relationships. Gemini was also used to assist in implementing and troubleshooting complex interactive elements.
